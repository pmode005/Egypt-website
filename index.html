Egypt is a land where the ancient past and a bustling modern pulse coexist along the banks of the Nile River. Often called the "Cradle of Civilization," it is home to some of humanity’s most iconic landmarks, including the Great Pyramids of Giza and the Great Sphinx, which serve as enduring symbols of the Pharaonic era’s architectural genius. Beyond its archaeological wonders, Egypt is a cultural powerhouse of the Arab world, with Cairo—its vibrant capital—acting as a center for cinema, music, and literature. From the serene Mediterranean beaches of Alexandria to the world-class diving reefs of the Red Sea, the country offers a diverse landscape tied together by the life-giving waters of the Nile and a heritage that has shaped history for millennia.
